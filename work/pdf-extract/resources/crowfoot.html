This narrative history of a Residential School was researched and produced by the Government of Canada as part of its response to litigation and the Indian Residential Schools Settlement Agreement. The National Centre for Truth and Reconciliation has not verified the content of this document. It is provided here for reference purposes only. Documented incidents of sexual and physical abuse are based on the documentary record and do not take into account survivor testimony.


You are welcome to contact the NCTR if you wish to add, comment on, or challenge any versions of the history presented herein.

DOCUMENTS REFERRING TO SCHOOL INCIDENTS

To date, we are unaware of any convictions for abuse at Crowfoot IRS or of any convicted abusers present at the school.


The following suggestions of abuse, listed chronologically, comprise all known identifiable complaints and/or allegations received by government officials and all available information regarding the follow-up and outcome:


Incidents (Physical)

A 1921 incident of chaining to benches and strapping leaving marks, referred to in a nurse=s report, led to instructions from the Indian Commissioner and from the Deputy Superintendent General that these forms of punishment would not be tolerated by the government and should be ended. The government intended to issue a circular on acceptable forms of punishment. [04206; 04206A;00158;04206B;04206C;04206D]
